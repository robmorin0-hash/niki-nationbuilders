System Wiring, Conduit, and Grounding for Off-Grid Systems

Grounded in primary-source research. Source: solar-pv-systems-design-installation-depth.

Proper wiring, conduit, and grounding are foundational to the reliability, safety, and efficiency of any off-grid solar or renewable energy system. Poor installation can lead to voltage drops, equipment damage, fire hazards, or even electrocution. This guide provides actionable steps for sizing conductors, selecting materials, and implementing grounding and lightning protection based on industry standards and practical experience.

DC Wiring from Array to Inverter

Voltage Drop and Conductor Sizing: Voltage drop is a critical factor in DC wiring. A general rule is to keep losses below 2–3% of the system voltage to avoid performance degradation. The formula to calculate wire gauge (in mm²) is: Wire gauge = (2 × ρ × I × L) / (V × %drop) Where ρ = copper resistivity (0.0173 Ω·mm²/m), I = current (A), L = one-way length (m), V = system voltage (typically 400V for large strings), and %drop = 0.02–0.03 (2–3%).

Example: For a 22A current, 30m run, 400V system, and 2% drop: (2 × 0.0173 × 22 × 30) / (400 × 0.02) = 22.8 / 8 = 2.85 mm². A #12 AWG copper wire (3.31 mm²) is standard and rated for 600V insulation.

PV Cable Specifications: Use cables with cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) or ethylene propylene rubber (EPR) insulation for durability. UV-resistant jackets are mandatory for outdoor exposure. Cables must be rated for 90°C nominal temperature (120°C for hot spots inside conduit) and 600V DC minimum. Higher-voltage strings (e.g., 1000V) require specialized cables, which cost 5–10× more than standard electrical wire.

Conduit and Routing: Select conduit based on environment: Schedule 40 PVC for burial/outdoor, Schedule 80 steel for high-traffic areas, and EMT for indoor use. Size conduit to accommodate conductor diameters:

  • Two #12 AWG conductors (3.3 mm each): Use 1/2" conduit (minimum 6.6 mm total diameter).
  • Four #2/0 AWG conductors (4 mm each): Use 2" conduit (16 mm total).

    • Burial depth must be at least 18" in residential areas, with 48–60" in cold climates to stay above frost.

AC Wiring from Inverter to Service Panel

Breaker and Disconnect Sizing: Breakers must match the inverter’s continuous output current. For example, a 5 kW inverter at 240V produces 20.8A continuous load. Use a 30A breaker (minimum #10 AWG Cu wire per NEC Table 310.15). A manual or automatic disconnect switch is required between the inverter and the service panel for maintenance.

AC Wiring Standards: Use standard electrical wire per NEC Article 310 (lower cost than PV cable). Voltage drop should remain under 3% for acceptable performance. Example: For a 20A load, 50ft run, 240V system, and 3% drop: (2 × 0.0173 × 20 × 15) / (240 × 0.03) = 10.4 / 7.2 = 1.44 mm². A #12 AWG Cu wire (3.31 mm²) is sufficient.

Grounding and Lightning Protection

Equipment Grounding (Safety): Install a dedicated ground rod (8 ft long, 5/8" diameter copper) driven to the frost line. Bond all DC equipment frames, AC enclosures, and service panels to the ground rod using #6–8 AWG bare copper conductors. Test the ground electrode system resistance to ensure it’s <25 Ω (preferably <10 Ω) for fault current dissipation.

Lightning Protection (SPDs): Use surge protective devices (SPDs) to mitigate lightning and transient surges:

  • Type 1 (coordinated): Installed at the utility meter to protect against distribution-line surges.
  • Type 2 (main): Placed at the inverter’s DC input and AC output for switching transients.
  • Type 3 (branch): Outlet-level SPDs for sensitive equipment.

    • SPDs use voltage-clamping (MOV, Zener diodes) or switching (spark gaps). Type 1/2 systems cost $300–$800.

DC-Side Lightning Protection: Install combiner boxes with DC surge protection between strings. Use SPDs rated for the array’s maximum Voc (e.g., 600V SPD for a 500V string). Ensure SPD turn-on voltage is 20% below the system voltage (e.g., 500V system uses 600V SPD with a 500 × 1.2 = 600V threshold).

Gear that Helps

  • PV cable: Cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) or ethylene propylene rubber (EPR) insulation, 600V DC minimum.
  • Conduit: Schedule 40 PVC for burial, Schedule 80 steel for high-traffic areas, EMT for indoor use.
  • AC wire: Standard electrical wire per NEC Article 310.
  • Ground rod: 8 ft long, 5/8" diameter copper rod.
  • SPDs: Type 1 (coordinated), Type 2 (main), Type 3 (branch) surge protective devices.
  • Combiner box: With DC surge protection between strings, rated for array Voc.
