How to Build a DIY LiFePO4 Battery Pack (Off-Grid Guide)

Grounded in primary-source research. Source: battery-designs-from-scratch-chemistry-build-depth.

LiFePO4 (LFP) batteries are the safest and most durable lithium chemistry for off-grid systems, offering 10–20 years of use at 1 cycle/day, a stable discharge voltage, and inherent thermal safety. For a 5–20 kWh home setup, a 4S (12V nominal) pack with 100–200 Ah capacity is ideal. This guide walks you through selecting cells, matching them, assembling the pack, and ensuring safety.

Cell Selection and Matching

Choose the right form factor:

  • 18650 cells (2.5–3.5 Ah, ~$5–15/cell) are cheap and abundant but harder to balance in parallel.
  • 21700 cells (4.0–5.0 Ah, pricier than 18650 with less recycled supply) are better for parallel configurations, reducing the number of cells needed.
  • Prismatic pouch cells (10–100 Ah, ~$15–50/Ah) offer high energy density but are fragile and costly.

For a 12V/100Ah pack (1.2 kWh), use 25 parallel strings of 4S (4 cells in series). This requires 100 cylindrical 21700 cells (4 Ah each), totaling 6.5 kg.

Matching criteria:

  • Internal resistance (IR): Select cells with IR within 2 mΩ (e.g., 10±2 mΩ). Use an ESR meter ($50–200) or battery analyzer ($300–1000) to measure.
  • Capacity: Ensure cells are within ±5% of rated capacity (e.g., 4.45–4.55 Ah). Test by discharging at 0.2C (0.9 A) to 2.5V cutoff using an adjustable DC load ($100–300).
  • Voltage: Pre-balance cells by charging/discharging at 0.1C (10% depth) to equalize OCV (open-circuit voltage). Select cells with OCV within ±0.05V (3.30–3.35V).

Pack Assembly Architecture

4S × 25P configuration (4 cells in series, 25 parallel branches):

  • Each 4S string provides 12.8V nominal (4 × 3.2V).
  • Connect 25 strings in parallel for 100 Ah capacity (4 Ah × 25).
  • Max voltage: 14.6V (4 × 3.65V).
  • Min voltage: 10V (4 × 2.5V).

Busbar design:

  • Use copper busbars (34 mm² cross-section, ~6 mm thick × 6 mm wide) for 100 A at <10 mV drop.
  • Aluminum is lighter and cheaper but risks oxidation.
  • Nickel-plated copper balances conductivity and corrosion resistance (~$10–20 per busbar).

Interconnection: Welding vs. Soldering

Spot welding (preferred for reliability):

  • Use a battery spot welder (40 V, 50 A, ~$100–300).
  • Clamp nickel-plated steel tabs to copper busbars and pulse 50 mJ energy (1–2 ms) for a 100 μΩ joint.
  • No flux or solder residue, minimal heat spread.

Soldering (less ideal but feasible):

  • Use lead-free SAC305 solder (217°C melt point) and a 60 W soldering iron.
  • Tin tabs and busbars, then join with heat. Avoid sustained >65°C to prevent cell degradation.
  • Solder joints (20–50 μΩ) are worse than welds, so limit to low-current packs (<20 A).

Recommendation: Invest in a spot welder for NIKI production packs. Kits ($50–200) are DIY-friendly with practice.

BMS (Battery Management System)

A BMS is critical for safety and longevity. It must: 1. Monitor voltage across 4 series cells (0–5V per cell). 2. Track temperature via thermistors (0–80°C). 3. Measure current using a 0.005 Ω precision shunt resistor. 4. Estimate state of charge by coulomb counting plus an open-circuit-voltage lookup. 5. Balance the four series cells (passive balancing uses 100–1000 Ω bleed resistors; active balancing transfers charge between cells).

Set the protection cutoffs to the LFP limits: stop charging if any cell exceeds 4.0V, disconnect the load if any cell drops below 2.5V, trip on overcurrent above 150 A (1.5C for a 100 Ah pack), and on overtemperature above 80°C. Ensure your BMS module is configured for LFP chemistry.

Essential Gear for Building Your LFP Pack

  • ESR meter ($50–200) for internal resistance testing.
  • Adjustable DC load ($100–300) for capacity verification.
  • Battery spot welder ($100–300) for reliable interconnections.
  • Nickel-plated copper busbars (~$10–20 per piece).
  • Thermistors for temperature sensing.
  • Shunt resistor (0.005 Ω precision) for current measurement.
  • BMS module with LFP-specific protection.

By following this guide, you’ll create a safe, durable LFP battery pack tailored for off-grid energy storage. Always prioritize safety during assembly and regular maintenance.
